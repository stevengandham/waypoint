# Waypoint

- **Meeting Frequency:** Monthly
- **Where:** Beachpoint Church
- **Catered to:** 25–32 yr olds

---

## Vision Statement

We recognize a critical gap in our community: a significant number of people in this age group around Beachpoint are past the traditional Young Adults programs yet remain unconnected to the consistent community and accountability provided by established Life Groups.

We do not want to replace life groups. Instead we want to create a non-intimidating "soft entry" point where people feel comfortable connecting to community and fellowship before they might feel comfortable joining a lifegroup. These events are a place to hang out and meet people in a similar life stage: a waypoint for people outside of the life of the church and church's existing programs.

---

## Beachpoint Value Alignment

### Transformational

We believe a community of connected believers in the same age bracket fosters growth and transformation.

### Relational

We believe the Christian walk is not meant to be walked alone. It is meant to be lived out in community. Genuine relationships are built brick by brick with intention. The aim of this ministry is to bring together individuals falling in the 25–32 age bracket, to spark friendships and create connections within the church body.

### Intergenerational

We believe in the importance of an intragenerational network within a church body in addition to Beachpoint's Intergenerational approach to community. Beachpoint 20-somethings are earnestly seeking a format for camaraderie, unity, and fellowship.

### Missional

We believe the initial trajectory for the Middle Ground ministry is to connect people together with the aim for organic growth and depth of relationship. There are numerous 20-somethings attending Beachpoint lacking the solace of friendship. Our mission is to connect them together in hopes they organically form their own friend groups and feel empowered to invite others to "do life with" them in the day to day.

### Generous

We believe breaking bread together is a powerful approach to fellowship (Acts 2:42). By practicing hospitality (often through self-funding and joint ownership of event costs), we create an environment where connection is prioritized, promoting a culture where every member is encouraged to open their life and home to others.
